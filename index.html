    Back at the table? The 20-second refresher

    • Deal: Blitz pile 10 · Post piles 3 (5 with two players) · rest in hand
    • Wood pile: flip your hand three cards at a time
    • Dutch piles: build 1 → 10 by color in the middle — anyone can play
    • Round ends: empty your Blitz pile, yell “Blitz!”
    • Score: +1 per Dutch card · −2 per Blitz leftover
    • Win: first to 75

    The objective

    Play as many of your cards as you can onto the shared center piles (the Dutch piles), and be the first to empty your Blitz pile. Emptying it ends the round — shout “Blitz!” and everyone stops instantly. First player to 75 points across rounds wins the game.

    Players & decks

    2–4 players with the standard set; the expansion pack takes it up to 8. Everyone plays simultaneously — there are no turns.

    Each player uses their own 40-card deck: numbers 1–10 in each of the four colors — red, blue, green, yellow. Decks are told apart by the picture on the back: plow, pail, pump, or carriage. Red and blue cards show a Dutch boy; yellow and green cards show a Dutch girl — that matters for Post piles.

    Pile glossary

    Blitz pile
    Your stack of 10 cards, face up, only the top card playable. Emptying it is the whole point — and ends the round.
    Post piles
    Your 3 face-up cards (working piles) next to the Blitz pile. In a 2-player game you use 5.
    Wood pile
    The rest of your deck, held in hand face down. You flip through it three cards at a time; only the top flipped card is playable.
    Dutch piles
    The shared piles in the middle of the table. Every 1 starts a new pile; each builds 1 → 10 in a single color. Cards played here score points.

    Setup

    1. Deal yourself a Blitz pile: 10 cards, face up, in one stack.
    2. Lay out 3 Post piles: one card each, face up (5 in a 2-player game).
    3. Hold the rest face down — that's your Wood pile.
    4. Leave the middle of the table clear for the Dutch piles.

    The gameplay loop

    1. Someone says go. Everybody plays at the same time, as fast as they can — with one hand placing cards.
    2. Any 1 you can play goes to the center to start a Dutch pile. Build Dutch piles upward, 1 → 10, same color only. Anyone can play on any Dutch pile.
    3. On your own Post piles, build downward — each card one lower than the card under it, and alternating boy/girl (red/blue on yellow/green and vice-versa).
    4. Fill an empty Post slot with the top card of your Blitz pile.
    5. No move? Flip the next three cards off your Wood pile. When the hand runs out, pick the flipped stack back up, turn it over, and keep going — no shuffling.
    6. The moment a player's Blitz pile is empty they shout “Blitz!” — all play stops immediately and the round is scored.

    Scoring

    • +1 point for every card you got onto the Dutch piles (sort the center piles by the deck-back picture to count).
    • −2 points for every card still in your Blitz pile.
    • Yes, negative rounds are real and happen to everyone.
    • Totals carry across rounds; first to 75 wins. (This app tracks all of that for you.)

    Edge cases

    • Mistake on the table? Any player who spots an illegal play calls “Dutch!” — the card goes back and play continues.
    • Only the top card of the Blitz pile, the flipped Wood stack, or a Post pile may be played. No digging.
    • Flipping the Wood pile other than in threes, or shuffling it mid-round, is not allowed.
    • Two cards race to the same Dutch pile? The one that lands first stays; the other goes back.
    • Everybody stuck with no plays? The boxed rulebook covers restart procedures — follow it for disputes rather than this summary.

    Variations

    • Casual target: play to a lower target (or a fixed number of rounds) for shorter games — this app lets you set any target.
    • Timed rounds: stop at a timer instead of a Blitz call, then score as usual.
    • Expansion pack: adds four more decks for 5–8 players. Chaos scales accordingly.

    Beginner cheat-sheet

    Seven things that separate “learning” from “losing politely.”

    1. Clear your Blitz pile first. Always. It's the only pile that punishes you (−2 a card). Every move should serve it.
    2. Don't tunnel on the Wood pile. Flipping threes feels productive. It isn't, if your Blitz card was playable the whole time.
    3. Scan the center constantly. Dutch piles change every second. The 4 you needed two seconds ago is buried now — but a new one just opened.
    4. Use Post piles to free Blitz cards. Can't play your Blitz card to the center? Park it on a Post pile (one lower, boy-on-girl) and unblock the pile.
    5. Keep your hands moving — legally. Speed wins rounds, but an illegal play gets a “Dutch!” called on you and costs more time than it saves.
    6. Negative rounds happen early. Accept them. A −6 while you learn the flow is tuition, not failure. The score tracker doesn't judge.
    7. Accuracy first, speed second. Fast and wrong loses to steady and clean. Speed comes on its own after a few rounds.
